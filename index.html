Frequently asked

Common questions about Silver Hoof Farm, our programs, and what to expect when you visit our equestrian center.

What riding levels do you accept?

We welcome riders of all levels — from complete beginners to advanced competitors. Our instructors tailor each lesson to your experience and goals, whether you're mounting for the first time or refining your dressage test. Youth horseback riding programs are a particular focus at Silver Hoof Farm.

What does boarding include?

Our full-care boarding includes a spacious stall with daily turnout, premium hay and grain, regular stall cleaning, access to our all-weather arenas, and on-site management. Blanketing, holding for farrier/vet, and supplemental feeding can be arranged. It's a home for your horse, not just a stable.

How do I book an event?

Hosting a clinic, show, or private gathering at Silver Hoof Farm is straightforward. Reach out via our contact page with your preferred dates and the nature of your event. We'll provide a custom quote for equestrian farm venue rental and walk you through our facilities. Luxury equestrian farm events in the US find a perfect setting here.

What should I wear for my first lesson?

Comfort and safety come first. Wear long pants (jeans or riding breeches are ideal) and boots with a small heel to prevent your foot from slipping through the stirrup. We provide ASTM-approved helmets for all students. Avoid loose, flapping clothing that might startle the horses.

Are there programs for children?

Absolutely. Our youth horseback riding programs introduce young riders to horsemanship in a safe, encouraging environment. From lead-line pony rides for the smallest visitors to our junior riding club and summer camp sessions, we nurture a lifelong love of horses at every age.
